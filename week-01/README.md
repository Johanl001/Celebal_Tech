## 🗒️ Notes & Key Takeaways

- **Descriptive vs Inferential Statistics**: Descriptive stats summarize data; inferential stats make predictions about a population from a sample.
- **PSI**: Values < 0.1 → No shift; 0.1–0.2 → Moderate shift; > 0.2 → Significant shift (model retraining likely needed).
- **PCA**: Reduces dimensionality while retaining maximum variance — built on eigendecomposition of the covariance matrix.
- **CLT**: Regardless of population distribution, sample means approach a normal distribution as sample size grows (n ≥ 30 rule of thumb).
